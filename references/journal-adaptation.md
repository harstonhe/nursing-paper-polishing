# Journal Adaptation Reference

Use this reference after identifying the target journal or publisher. Always prioritize user-provided instructions and current official author guidelines over this built-in summary.

## Decision Rules

1. Detect the target journal and publisher from the user prompt, manuscript file, title page, or URL.
2. If the target is named, verify current official author guidelines when possible.
3. Extract only the requirements relevant to the requested task.
4. Apply language standards consistently, but keep format, abstract headings, reference style, and submission items journal-specific.
5. State any unresolved formatting uncertainty instead of guessing.

## Built-In Profiles

### IJNS and Similar Elsevier Nursing Journals

Use `references/ijns-requirements.md` for IJNS. For other Elsevier journals, consult the specific ScienceDirect Guide for Authors before applying IJNS-only items.

Do not assume all Elsevier journals require:

- IJNS article highlights
- IJNS abstract headings
- Harvard author-date references
- the same word limits

Elsevier publisher-level baseline:

- Consult the target journal's Guide for Authors for word limits, structure, formatting, references, and submission requirements.
- Some Elsevier journals use `Your Paper Your Way`, meaning initial submissions may allow any consistent reference style and flexible formatting.
- Generative AI use must be transparent and human-checked where disclosure is required.
- Data statements may be requested depending on journal policy.

### JMIR Publications

JMIR is a publisher family with a strong house style. Verify the specific JMIR journal and article type.

Common JMIR profile for Original Papers and Reviews:

- Abstract: structured, maximum 450 words.
- Typical abstract headings: `Background`, `Objective`, `Methods`, `Results`, `Conclusions`.
- Keywords: usually 5-10 keywords or key phrases, separated by semicolons; prefer MeSH terms.
- Main text: usually IMRD headings, `Introduction`, `Methods`, `Results`, `Discussion`.
- Style: follow JMIR House Style and AMA Manual of Style, 11th edition.
- Formatting at initial submission: font and size may not be critical; Word default heading styles are acceptable.
- Tables: use Word's built-in table feature, portrait orientation, sequential numbering, no shading or coloring, captions directly after first mention, and abbreviation definitions in captions or footnotes.
- RCTs and systematic reviews need registration details when applicable.
- Reporting guideline checklists may be required for some study designs.

JMIR article types vary. Research Letters, Viewpoints, Tutorials, Implementation Reports, Protocols, and other article types may have different abstract structures, headings, word limits, and tables/figures limits.

### Wiley Journals

Wiley journal formatting is journal-specific.

Publisher-level baseline:

- Most Wiley journals offer free-format submission, but the journal's Author Guidelines on Wiley Online Library control specific requirements.
- Supported main document formats may include `.docx`, `.tex`, or compressed LaTeX archives, depending on the journal.
- Some journals require a significance statement or plain language summary.
- Wiley research and synthesis articles commonly require a data availability statement.
- Wiley journals may use single-anonymized or double-anonymized review. For double-anonymized review, upload a separate title page and remove author-identifying information from the main manuscript.

### Wiley Journal Example: Journal of Advanced Nursing

Treat `Journal of Advanced Nursing` as one specific Wiley journal with its own author guidelines, not as a publisher-level category. Do not rely on IJNS, JMIR, or generic Wiley formatting.

Before final formatting or abstract restructuring:

- Retrieve the current `Journal of Advanced Nursing` Author Guidelines from Wiley Online Library or use the user's uploaded JAN template/guidelines.
- Verify article category, abstract headings, word limits, title page requirements, impact/significance statement requirements, references, tables/figures, data availability, and anonymization.
- If current guidelines cannot be accessed, use only general nursing SCI language polishing and flag journal-specific formatting as unresolved.

Common JAN-facing writing priorities to check after guideline verification:

- International relevance of the nursing problem.
- Clear distinction between `Introduction`, `Background`, `Methods`, `Results`, `Discussion`, `Limitations`, `Conclusion`, and any `Impact` or practice relevance section requested by the journal.
- Strong but restrained explanation of how the work may affect nursing practice, education, management, policy, or research.
- No causal or practice-changing claims unless the design supports them.

### Unknown Target Journal

If no journal is named:

- Use general nursing SCI style.
- Keep formatting neutral: editable Word text, Times New Roman 12 pt, double spacing, consistent headings, complete references, and clear tables/figures.
- Do not impose IJNS highlights, JMIR AMA style, or Wiley significance statements.
- Ask for the target journal only if the user requests final submission formatting.
