# IJNS Requirements Reference

Sources: International Journal of Nursing Studies Guide for Authors, ScienceDirect, accessed 2026-05-19; IJNS manuscript template provided by the user, footer dated August 2024.

## Journal Scope and Editorial Fit

- IJNS publishes original research and scholarship about health care delivery, organisation, management, workforce, policy, and research methods relevant to nursing, midwifery, and related health professions.
- The journal values evidence-informed policy and practice, rigorous designs appropriate to the question, and work that reduces important uncertainty or raises important new questions.
- Reviews and discussion papers must engage critically with a substantive body of research or scholarship.
- Instrument development or validation papers need strong practical usefulness; the full instrument should be available as supplementary material when required.

## Article Types and Length

- Research papers: 2,000-7,000 words, abstract up to 400 words.
- Reviews and discussion papers: 2,000-7,000 words, abstract up to 400 words.
- Editorials: up to 1,000 words, no abstract.
- Letters/comments/responses: up to 1,000 words, no abstract.

## Title and Front Matter

- Recommended title format: `Topic: Method/design`.
- Include a short article title in the manuscript header.
- Use given names followed by family names; omit titles and qualifications.
- Separate title pages from the anonymized manuscript for double-anonymized review.
- Delete instruction text, italicized template headings, examples, blank pages, and redundant sections before submission.
- Include acknowledgments only when needed.
- Include funding information in the standard Elsevier format, or state that no specific funding was received.

## Abstract

General requirements:

- Maximum 400 words.
- Include an optional social media abstract of up to 140 characters when desired.
- Use `Social media abstract:` with a colon, not a dash, and do not italicize the heading.
- Stand-alone, concise, factual.
- Avoid references unless essential.
- Avoid non-standard abbreviations and define essential abbreviations at first mention.

Research paper template:

- `Background`
- `Objective`
- `Design`
- `Setting(s)`
- `Participants`
- `Methods`
- `Results`
- `Conclusions`
- `Registration` when relevant

Review template:

- `Background`
- `Objective`
- `Information sources`
- `Methods`
- `Results`
- `Conclusions`
- `Registration` when relevant

Statistical results in the abstract should include point estimates and confidence intervals when possible. Conclusions must follow from the Results.

## Keywords and Highlights

- Provide 1-7 keywords.
- Prefer MeSH or CINAHL terms when possible.
- Arrange keywords alphabetically and separate them with semicolons when preparing IJNS-style submission files.
- Article highlights use up to five bullet points total under:
  - `What is already known`
  - `What this paper adds`
- Highlight bullets should be single sentences, usually two or three per heading, and no more than 85 characters when preparing final submission materials.

## Manuscript Body

Common structure:

- `Background`
- `Methods`
- `Results`
- `Discussion`
- `Limitations`
- `Conclusions`
- `References`

Methods for research papers should include ethics approval, informed consent, and study registration where relevant. Reviews should include registration where relevant.

## Formatting Defaults for IJNS Submission Preparation

Use these as a consistency standard when the user asks for Word formatting, submission readiness, or table/figure formatting. Treat them as a house style unless a stricter journal, institutional, or template instruction is supplied.

Main manuscript:

- Font: Times New Roman throughout the manuscript.
- Main text size: 12 pt.
- Line spacing: double-spaced for abstract, main text, references, acknowledgments, funding, and declarations.
- Layout: single-column Word-compatible editable text.
- Alignment: left-aligned or justified consistently; avoid mixed alignment within the same section.
- Margins: keep margins consistent, preferably standard 1 inch or 2.54 cm margins when no template margin is specified.
- Paragraphs: use consistent first-line indent or block spacing, not both in the same section.
- Headings: use a clear hierarchy and consistent capitalization; avoid decorative styles.
- Page numbering: keep page numbers consistent across manuscript files.
- Header: include the short article title where the IJNS template requests it.
- Template cleanup: remove all instruction text, sample text, redundant sections, and unused optional headings.

Title page:

- Keep author names, affiliations, corresponding author details, author footnotes, social media handles, acknowledgments, and funding in the template order.
- Do not include academic degrees or professional titles in author names.

Tables:

- Use editable text, not images.
- Use Times New Roman consistently.
- Table title: 12 pt, placed above the table, numbered in order of citation.
- Table body: 10-11 pt, selected consistently across all tables.
- Table notes: 10 pt, placed below the table.
- Avoid vertical lines and unnecessary shading.
- Use consistent decimal places, units, confidence interval formatting, and p-value formatting.
- Define all abbreviations and statistical symbols in table notes.
- Do not repeat all table values in the Results prose.

Figures:

- Figure captions: 12 pt Times New Roman, placed below figures unless the submission system separates captions.
- Figure labels and panel letters: readable and consistent, usually 8-10 pt after final sizing.
- Axis labels, legends, and annotations: readable after reduction, usually 8-10 pt, with consistent font family.
- Use high-resolution, non-pixelated artwork.
- Avoid overcrowded labels, low-contrast colors, and decorative effects.
- Define abbreviations, symbols, and error bars in the caption or legend.
- Ensure each figure is cited in the text before or near its first appearance.

Captions and numbering:

- Use `Table 1`, `Table 2`, `Fig. 1`, `Fig. 2`, or the manuscript's consistent style.
- Keep title/caption punctuation consistent.
- For supplementary items, use wording such as `supplementary material table X` and define numbering separately when needed.

## Reporting and Statistics

- Use the appropriate EQUATOR reporting guideline for the design.
- Randomized trials should follow CONSORT and include a flow diagram.
- Statistical reporting should not rely only on p values. Prefer point estimates, confidence intervals, effect sizes, and practical or clinical interpretation.
- Avoid treating statistical significance as the primary criterion for importance.

## Tables, Figures, and Supplementary Material

- Use no more than five tables and figures unless justified.
- Cite every table, figure, and supplementary item in the text.
- Cite supplementary tables and figures using wording such as `supplementary material table X`.
- Do not duplicate table content in prose.
- Avoid vertical rules and shading in tables.
- Define abbreviations in table or figure notes.

## Abbreviations

- Avoid abbreviations unless they are universally recognizable to a general international audience, SI units, common tests, or common statistical terms.
- Never include abbreviations in titles or abstracts unless firmly established and unavoidable.
- Define abbreviations used in tables and figures in a note.

## References

- Submission does not require strict reference formatting, but references must be complete and consistent.
- IJNS applies an author-date style after acceptance.
- Include DOI where available.
- Avoid citing large groups of references for a single point; use a few illustrative examples or refer to tables in reviews.
- Letters/comments/responses have a maximum of 10 references.

## Ethics and AI Disclosure

- Follow human-subject ethics, informed consent, privacy, and patient-detail requirements.
- Declare funding and competing interests.
- If generative AI or AI-assisted tools were used beyond basic grammar, spelling, or reference checking, include the required declaration before the reference list.

## Letters, Comments, Responses, and Editorials

- Letters/comments/responses are up to 1,000 words and have no abstract.
- Comment titles use `Comment: Comment on "article title"`.
- Response titles use `Response: Author's response to "article title"`.
- Letters and comments start with `Dear Editor` and include the heading `Letter`.
- Author details are placed at the end of letters/comments/responses.
- Editorials are up to 1,000 words and no abstract is required.
- Editorial titles should include `editorial`; omit the salutation to the editor.

## IJNS-Specific Language Implications

- Emphasize international nursing relevance, methodological appropriateness, and uncertainty reduction.
- Keep conclusions close to the study design and data.
- For descriptive or correlational studies, use cautious implication language rather than causal or effectiveness language.
- For reviews, distinguish evidence mapping from intervention evaluation.
