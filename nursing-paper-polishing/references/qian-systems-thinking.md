# Qian Xuesen-Inspired Systems Thinking for Manuscript Revision

This reference stores general reasoning logic inspired by Qian Xuesen's engineering cybernetics, systems engineering, open complex giant systems, and qualitative-to-quantitative meta-synthesis methodology. Use it only as a workflow scaffold for nursing manuscript writing and revision. Do not store or infer any user's actual manuscript topic, data, reviewer comments, or private project details here.

Sources consulted include public summaries and scholarly discussions of Qian Xuesen's engineering cybernetics, systems science, open complex giant systems, and qualitative-to-quantitative comprehensive integration methodology, including Chinese Academy of Engineering articles and public educational materials. This file paraphrases methodology only.

## Core Translation to Manuscript Work

Treat a manuscript as a controlled evidence system:

- `Control goal`: the target section's job, target journal fit, reviewer demand, or submission requirement.
- `Inputs`: data, tables, figures, protocol details, search records, citations, author notes, and target journal rules.
- `State`: the current draft, including what is clear, missing, overclaimed, redundant, or poorly connected.
- `Constraints`: study design, reporting guideline, ethics, word limit, reference style, and journal conventions.
- `Feedback`: reviewer comments, exemplar article patterns, guideline checklists, and internal consistency checks.
- `Output`: revised prose that reduces uncertainty and improves traceability from evidence to claim.

## Useful Logic

### 1. Engineering control loop

For each revision, ask:

1. What is the control goal?
2. What evidence input supports it?
3. What error signal exists in the current draft?
4. What correction reduces the error without creating new overclaiming?
5. What feedback check confirms the correction?

Use this for reviewer comments, missing Methods details, unclear Results reporting, and over-strong Discussion claims.

### 2. From practice to theory and back

Qian's engineering control view emphasizes extracting theory from practical engineering problems and improving theory through repeated application. In manuscript work:

- start from the author's actual data and study conduct;
- abstract the section logic and journal pattern;
- rewrite the section;
- check whether the rewrite remains faithful to the evidence;
- revise again if the wording hides uncertainty or creates unsupported claims.

### 3. System boundary before local edits

Before editing sentences, define the system boundary:

- Which section is being revised?
- What evidence can this section use?
- Which claims belong elsewhere?
- Which journal or reporting rules constrain it?
- What should not be solved in this section?

This prevents Results from becoming Discussion, Discussion from inventing Results, and Abstracts from exceeding what Methods and Results support.

### 4. Hierarchy and subsystem coordination

A manuscript has nested subsystems:

- title and abstract;
- Introduction/Background;
- Methods;
- Results;
- Discussion;
- Limitations;
- Conclusion;
- tables, figures, supplementary materials, and references.

Each subsystem has its own job, but all must align. A change in eligibility criteria may require updates to search strategy, flow diagram, extraction, Results counts, limitations, and abstract.

### 5. Qualitative-to-quantitative comprehensive integration

For complex manuscripts, integrate:

- qualitative judgment: journal style, reviewer concerns, conceptual gap, clinical relevance;
- quantitative or factual anchors: sample size, dates, databases, estimates, counts, table values, search records;
- expert or exemplar input: official guidelines and full-text articles from the target journal.

Do not let qualitative rhetoric outrun quantitative or factual anchors. Do not let tables remain uninterpreted without a section-level message.

### 6. Human-machine synthesis

Use AI as a structured assistant, not as the source of manuscript truth:

- AI can identify missing links, propose structure, polish wording, and check consistency.
- Authors must verify facts, data, eligibility rules, citations, ethics details, and interpretations.
- When author evidence is missing, output `AUTHOR_INPUT_NEEDED` instead of filling the gap.

### 7. Open complex system humility

Nursing, health services, digital health, caregiver support, and implementation contexts behave like open complex systems. Therefore:

- avoid universal claims;
- state boundary conditions;
- distinguish feasibility, usability, acceptability, association, mapping, and effectiveness;
- treat context, culture, workforce, resource constraints, and equity as possible modifiers.

### 8. Feedback memory without private content

At the end of a work session, update the skill only with:

- reusable workflow rules;
- generic writing patterns;
- non-sensitive examples;
- better checklists or guardrails.

Never store:

- the user's real manuscript topic;
- unpublished findings;
- reviewer identities or private comments;
- dataset details;
- institution-specific confidential information.

## Compact Checklist

Use this checklist when a revision feels messy:

1. Define the control goal.
2. List evidence inputs.
3. Name constraints.
4. Locate the draft error.
5. Choose the smallest correction.
6. Check claim-evidence-boundary alignment.
7. Propagate necessary changes to related sections.
8. Record only generic learning in the skill.
